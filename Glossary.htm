A

Asymptomatic

Without symptoms

C

Congenital

Present at birth.

CSF

Cerebrospinal Fluid - Fluid surrounding the brain and spinal cord.

Back to Top

D

Dura Mater

The outermost layer of the meninges

Back to Top

E

Electrocautery

Using high frequency electrical impulses to destroy tissue.

Back to Top

F

Foramen Magnum

The funnel-like opening at the base of the skull that connects the cranium to the spinal canal.

Fossa

A depression or hollow, usually in a bone.

Back to Top

H

Hydrocephalus

Abnormal accumulation of CSF in the skull.

Hydromyelia

Dilation of the central canal of the spinal cord due to excess CSF accumulation.

Back to Top

I

Incision

A surgical cut made in skin or flesh.

Back to Top

L

Lamina

Part of the vertebra that forms the roof of the spinal canal.

Laminectomy

Removal of all or part of the lamina.

Back to Top

M

Meninges

Membranes that envelop the brain and spinal cord.

Myelomeningocele

Spinal defect where a portion of the spinal meninges protrudes through a gap in the bony spinal column.

Back to Top

P

Perforation

A hole made by boring or piercing

Pia Mater

The layer on the meninges closest to the brain and spinal cord.

Posterior

Referring to the back of the body

Back to Top

S

Subarachnoid Space

The space between the dura mater and the pia mater.

Syringomyelia

A disorder that results in a fluid-filled cyst forming in the spinal canal.

Syrinx

Fluid-filled cyst in the spinal canal.

Back to Top

V

Ventricle

A hollow part of cavity in an organ. In this case, it refers to hollow spaces in the center of the brain.

Ventriculostomy

A procedure to insert a drain tube into the ventricles of the brain.
